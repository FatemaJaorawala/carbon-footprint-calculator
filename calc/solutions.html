4 Technologies to Reduce Carbon Emission and Promote Sustainable Development

Sustainable development and carbon emission reduction are key targets for industries today. New innovations aim to create carbon-neutral materials and green-fueled technologies. Below are four key technologies that are helping to meet emission reduction targets.

1. Zero-Carbon Cement

University of Colorado Boulder engineers are using microalgae to make carbon-neutral cement. Cement production is responsible for 7% of global greenhouse gas emissions. The team aims to replace traditional limestone with calcium carbonate derived from photosynthetic microalgae called coccolithophores, which naturally store carbon.

2. Photosynthesizing Hydrogen

Scientists at the University of Cambridge and Imperial College London have created a device that generates hydrogen from water using sunlight. This "artificial leaf" can split water into oxygen and hydrogen for 240 hours, without any additional energy input, potentially leading to green hydrogen production.

3. Carbon Trapper

Researchers at the University of Cambridge developed a coin-sized supercapacitor made from coconut shells and saltwater that can capture CO2 from the air. By improving the charging process, this device absorbs carbon more efficiently, potentially offering a cheaper and more sustainable alternative to current carbon capture methods.

4. 100% Sustainable Aviation Fuel

GE engineers have tested their Passport engine using 100% sustainable aviation fuel (SAF). This fuel-efficient technology could play a key role in helping the aviation industry achieve net-zero CO2 emissions by 2050.
